🔬 Excited to share my first research project:
**Transcriptomic Analysis of Glucotoxic Human Podocytes**

As part of my work at the Department of Biotechnology, REVA University, I conducted a full end-to-end bioinformatics study on diabetic kidney disease (DKD) — analysing how sustained high glucose rewires gene expression in human podocytes.

---

🧬 **What I did:**
Performed a multi-layered secondary analysis of public RNA-seq data (GSE307956) — human podocytes cultured under high (30 mM), medium (15 mM), and normal (5.5 mM) glucose conditions — using:

✅ DESeq2 differential expression analysis
✅ GO & KEGG pathway enrichment (ORA + GSEA)
✅ STRINGdb protein–protein interaction network analysis
✅ Transcription factor activity inference (decoupleR / DoRothEA)
✅ External validation in human DKD glomerular tissue (GSE30528)

---

🔑 **Key Findings:**

▶ Identified **728 conserved core DEGs** shared across both glucose-reduction contrasts
▶ **Ribosomal pathway** was the most strongly enriched (~76% of KEGG-annotated ribosomal genes in the core set) — pointing to broad translational activation under glucotoxic stress
▶ **NOMO3** emerged as the single most significantly differentially expressed gene (padj < 10⁻²⁵⁰), marking a novel and previously unreported link between ER membrane dysfunction and podocyte glucotoxicity
▶ **Mitochondrial remodelling** confirmed via oxidative phosphorylation enrichment and MRPL hub gene activation
▶ **TF activity analysis** revealed suppression of ESR1, HIF1A, FOXO3, and SMAD3 — transcriptional regulators critical for podocyte identity and metabolic homeostasis
▶ Ribosomal signatures (RPS15, RPS19, RPL13) validated in independent human DKD tissue data

---

💡 **Why it matters:**
This integrated approach pinpoints ER stress and ribosomal dysfunction as early and critical drivers in diabetic kidney disease — opening potential directions for mechanistic studies and therapeutic targets.
